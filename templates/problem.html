Environmental Impact

The consequences of oil spills extend far beyond immediate contamination

Marine Life Devastation

Oil coats feathers and fur, destroying animals' natural insulation and buoyancy. Marine mammals and birds often die from hypothermia, starvation, or drowning.

Ecosystem Collapse

Oil toxicity disrupts entire food chains, killing plankton and small organisms that form the base of marine ecosystems, leading to long-term biodiversity loss.

Human & Community Health

Coastal communities exposed to oil spills suffer from respiratory issues and water contamination. Cleanup workers face long-term health hazards.
